What is compound interest?

Compound interest means you earn interest not just on your original deposit, but also on the interest you've already earned. Over time, this creates a snowball effect where your money grows faster and faster — this is purely educational context to help you understand how money can work.

Simple interest: £1,000 at 7% = £70 per year, every year.
Compound interest: £1,000 → £1,070 → £1,144.90 → £1,225.04…

The Rule of 72

A rough way to estimate how long it takes to double your money: divide 72 by your expected annual return rate. This is an educational tool to illustrate the concept — not a guarantee of any returns.

At 6%: 72 ÷ 6 = ~12 years to double.
At 8%: 72 ÷ 8 = ~9 years to double.
At 12%: 72 ÷ 12 = ~6 years to double.

This is why starting early matters — time in the market is one of the biggest variables in long-term wealth building.
